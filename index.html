Hate Crimes in the United States

By Romy Ellenbogen

Source: FBI

Hate Crimes in 2016 by type of bias

This chart shows hate crimes, as reported to the FBI from local police agencies. Though there are other crimes based on bias, like on Anti-Arab sentiment or anti-multiracial sentiment, the majority of crimes happen to either white or black individuals. For nearly every race, intimidation was the most common crime, except for those crimes committed because of Anti-White sentiment. Black people far and away faced the most hate crimes. With constant discussions of race in the country, this data shows how many times a certain group is targeted for no reason other than bias against their race or ethnicity. Something to be considered is that not every police agency will accurately report and the difference in population sizes among racial groups in the United States.

Source: FBI

Hate crimes reported in 2016 by state

This chart shows how many incidents of a hate crime there were reported in each state to the FBI. Reported is the key word, because not all police agencies may consider a certain crime a hate crime. Because of this, the numbers may not be truly reflective of the entire situation. Only a certain number of police agencies report numbers as well. Not unsurprisingly, states with higher populations had a large amount of reports, with no clear geographical concentration of hate crimes.
